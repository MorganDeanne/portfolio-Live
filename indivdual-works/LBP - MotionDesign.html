The Little Black Pen

A project for looking into the trend of motion design for product selling

In 2021, I had a module for University called “Portfolio Project”, where our tutor took a module to fill in any knowledge gaps. I chose to do a “Still life animated website” to sell any product in your own house. The only criteria was creating your own content - both text and images - and using motion design was encouraged.
This seemed a little more creative than previous works, so I decided to give this a go.

To start off, I researched a bit about motion design in general. I looked at some sites and one of wich I fell in love with design. If you visit sakarisake.com, then you are greated with a beautiful website, full of rich history and glorious designs, As you scroll, more informaiton is given and is displayed in a satifying but not to jarring way.

Seeing the expectation, It was quite daunting, however I was eager to get started

So I started to think, what is something everyone has in thier home? A pen. Everyone has that one pen that’s always knocking about. So picked up my own black pen and took various photos of it (here are some of the photos I took). I also went into GIMP 2.0 and edited the pen to be two seperate parts, the barrel and the lid.

With these images, I got thinking into the necessary content for the pen. I needed a title which I ended up on “Little Black Pen” as it is an essenctial item in every home that small but mighty!

With the title, I chose a font that looked like it was wrote with the exact pan. I created some information on the pen, like the barrel size and the colour. The next section is were I was inspired by the sakari website. I wanted the pen to be floating and I loved the texture of the napkin behind the bottle of sake. So with this in mind, my design featured a the pen, which upon scrolling, the lid would collapse onto the pen, and the paper texture in the background would slide behind the pen.

The photos would slide in from the sides adjacent to eachother. I then added some small little reviews of the pen, which used the same font as the title to tie it all in.
